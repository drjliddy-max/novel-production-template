# Book Description Prompt

Use this prompt to draft Amazon book descriptions, back-cover blurbs, KDP listing copy, or any other short-form book marketing copy in John Liddy's voice. This is the form that converts browsers into buyers, so it has to do real work in a small space.

## Prompt for the AI

You are drafting book description copy for John Liddy. Read these files first, before drafting a single word:

- `style-guide/JOHN_LIDDY_VOICE.md` — voice rules, non-negotiable
- `BOOK_METADATA_TEMPLATE.md` — book metadata format
- This file — description-specific conventions

Book descriptions are a hybrid form. They need to be honest enough to feel like the author's actual voice, and specific enough to convert a curious reader into a buyer. Most Amazon descriptions are 150-300 words for the main block, plus a shorter back-cover version of 75-150 words.

### Book specifics to fill in

- **Title:** [e.g. "The Participation Effect"]
- **Subtitle:** [e.g. "A Practical Framework for Navigating Uncertainty"]
- **Author display name:** [e.g. "Dr. John A. Liddy, D.C."]
- **Genre:** [e.g. "Personal development / Practical philosophy"]
- **Target reader (one sentence):** [who is searching Amazon and would buy this]
- **Reader pain point:** [the specific frustration they're carrying when they find this book]
- **Reader's prior failed attempts:** [what they've already tried that didn't work]
- **Core promise of the book:** [one sentence]
- **Differentiator from competing books:** [what makes this not just another self-help title]
- **Proof points:** [reviews, credentials, lived experience]
- **Companion or follow-on:** [e.g. Daily Rise app, free chapter]

### Description structure

**Hook (first sentence).** Names the reader's specific frustration or situation. Not abstract. Specific enough that the reader thinks "this is for me." Examples that work for John's voice:

- "If you've already tried therapy, manifestation, journaling, and three different productivity systems and you still feel stuck, this book is for you."
- "Most self-help books promise you'll feel better. This one shows you why you couldn't think clearly in the first place."

**Problem block (2-3 sentences).** Names the specific failure mode of mainstream self-help, the way the reader's nervous system actually works, or the trap they keep falling into. Concrete. Specific. Resists abstraction.

**Promise block (2-3 sentences).** What this book actually delivers. Not "transformation" or "your best self." Specific outcomes. The framework. The daily practice. The shift.

**Credibility block (2-3 sentences).** Who the author is and why they have the right to write this book. For John specifically: 20+ years of rehab practice on Sunset Strip in West Hollywood, his own crisis and recovery, the framework that came out of it. Not bragging. Earned.

**Close (1-2 sentences).** The line that converts. Either a direct invitation ("Read the first chapter free below") or a sharp framing ("This isn't a self-help book. It's an operating system.").

### Voice rules

The standard voice rules from `style-guide/JOHN_LIDDY_VOICE.md` all apply. For book descriptions specifically:

- Open with the reader's actual situation, not with a meta-statement about the book
- Negate before revealing: "Not another self-help book. A framework that works."
- Specific vocabulary throughout: name the actual problem, the actual practice, the actual outcome
- Short sentences carry the weight in this form. Not every sentence, but the punch lines.
- No em dashes
- No "However," "Furthermore," "Moreover"
- No abstract benefit language ("transformation," "your best self," "unlock")

### Banned phrases for John's books

These phrases are non-negotiable removals for any book by John A. Liddy:

- "Manifest" / "manifestation"
- "The universe delivers" / "the universe responds"
- "Vibration" / "raise your vibration"
- "Law of attraction"
- "Source"
- "Broadcasting"
- "Alignment" (in the metaphysical sense)
- "Find your why"
- "Live your best life"

### Required language for The Participation Effect specifically

The Amazon description for *The Participation Effect* should use this language at least once:

- Participation
- Probability (or "probability paths")
- Perception
- Emotional regulation
- Clarity
- Recognition
- Practical framework
- Practice (referring to the daily practice)

This matches the book's actual thesis and the keywords the SEO targets.

### Self-check before returning the draft

1. Does the first sentence name a real reader frustration, not a meta-statement?
2. Are there any banned phrases?
3. Is the credibility block specific (numbers, places, lived experience), not generic?
4. Does the close convert, or just trail off?
5. Are there any em dashes or banned transitions?
6. Does it sound like John, or like a marketing copywriter?
7. For Amazon specifically: is there a clear, scannable structure that reads well in the limited Amazon listing format?

If any fail, rewrite before returning.

## Variants

### Back-cover blurb (75-150 words)

Use the same structure as the Amazon description but compressed. Cut the credibility block to one sentence. Cut the problem block to one sentence. Keep the hook, the promise, and the close.

### One-line elevator pitch (under 20 words)

Use this format: "[Genre] for [reader] who wants [outcome] without [common rejection]." Example: "Practical philosophy for tired adults who want clarity without the woo."

### Author bio (50-150 words)

Lead with the surprising credential. For John: "Dr. John A. Liddy spent two decades treating recovering addicts in his rehab practice on the Sunset Strip in West Hollywood." Then: lived experience, the framework, the books. End with an invitation to the work.
